Honour Your Loved Ones at Your Wedding

PLANNING + ADVICE


Image Source: Strand of Silk

We lost our grandpa a year ago, and as we get closer to the anniversary, all these memories of him talking about my wedding “someday”, keep coming back to me. He had a lot of friends. So, we would joke that there would be more of his friends at my wedding than my own. He was also very old-school, so it’s a safe bet that he would have expected all the traditions to be followed and done by the book.

While it’s heartbreaking that he cannot be there to watch me walk down the aisle, I started penning a list of ways I could include his memory at my wedding.

If you are in the same boat and are looking to honor a lost one—whether you want to do something big, or something subtle, here are a couple ideas I’ve compiled:


In Your Jewellery/Carry a Family Heirloom

Was there a signature piece of jewellery that your loved one was known to always wear? Or maybe something they wore at their own wedding?Incorporate this heirloom piece into your wedding day ensemble.

Whether it was a broach, or a simple necklace that you can remember them wearing, include it on your wedding day, so you’ll feel like they’re right there with you.


In the Food

You could do something small and subtle like simply adding their favourite Gulab Jamun to the menu, or, you could go all out and make the entire days theme based on their favourite cuisine. Were they always partial to Italian food? Have live pizza and pasta counters at your reception, followed by rich, decadent Tiramisu for dessert.

I saw that my friend did something similar for his wedding, so I talked to him about it. He said:


“Papa was always the life of the party,
and a whiskey man through and through.
So, for my Sangeet, we had the bartender create a whiskey cocktail
because not many people like to drink straight whiskey!”
—Aryaman, 28

Reserve a Seat

Usually the front few rows are unofficially “reserved” for close relatives. Or in my family, we usually have the parents, and grandparents on the mandap with the bride and groom during the main wedding ceremony or “Muhurtham”.

Leave a seat open, either on the mandap, or in the front row, and place a picture of who you want to honour, or a bouquet of flowers on it.

It’ll be nice to look out in the crowd and see their smiling photo where they would have been.


Make a Donation to Charity

In lieu of gifts, have guests directly donate money or essential items to an organisation that your loved one was passionate about. Or have them donate to an organisation you are passionate about in your loved one’s name.

There’s also a TON of leftover food after each wedding event. Instead of wasting it, have local organisations and temples that feed the poor or homeless, come and pick it up. However, there might be some restrictions in what food they are allowed to collect. So, don’t forget to consult the organisations while you’re planning the menus!


Make a Special Toast

If you want to do something more conspicuous, make a quick toast in front of your guests. Say a few words about your loved one—maybe advice they imparted, or how much they meant to you.

It can be as simple as saying, “This song is for___” and play their favourite tune at your sangeet.


Try to Recreate the Past

Go through old photos and see if you could recreate how their wedding was. Maybe incorporate the same flowers or wear the same colour saree they wore on their special day.

It’s tough when someone that means so much to you is not around. Especially on what’s supposed to be one of the happiest days of your life. By including elements that remind you of them, it should feel like they are right there by your side.
